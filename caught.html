You got caught

Scammers rely on habit-clicking. “I Agree” buttons are designed to make you move fast and think later.

What happened? You clicked through terms quickly without reviewing what you were accepting. In real situations, this can expose your personal data, approve tracking, or lead to hidden redirects and fake services.

Common scam techniques

  • Urgency: “Act now”, “Continue”, “Limited time”
  • Professional design: clean UI to earn trust quickly
  • Too good to be true: guaranteed results, free money, instant fixes
  • Unclear terms: vague permissions and hidden tracking

10-second safety checklist

  • Preview the URL before you click.
  • Ask: “What am I giving up if I accept?”
  • Search the site name + “reviews” or “scam”.
  • Use 2FA on important accounts.
