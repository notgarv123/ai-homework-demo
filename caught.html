Why scammers love “I Agree” buttons

Many people click fast without reading. Scammers rely on urgency, trust, and habits. Even professional-looking pages can hide risky actions.

What to watch for (real-world red flags)

  • Urgency: “Act now”, “Continue”, “Limited time”
  • Too-good-to-be-true: free money, instant results, “guaranteed” claims
  • Vague terms: unclear data usage, no transparency
  • Unnecessary permissions: contacts, files, messages, or “full access”
  • Hidden redirects: a button that sends you to a different site

10-second safety checklist

  • Preview the link/URL before clicking.
  • Ask: “What am I giving up if I agree?”
  • Search the site/service name + “scam” or “reviews.”
  • Use 2FA on important accounts.
